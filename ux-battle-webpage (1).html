UX Battle: Zerodha vs. Dhan vs. Upstox

An in-depth analysis of India's top trading platforms through the lens of user experience.

Introduction

In India's booming retail investment space, trading platforms have become utilities — not just tools. However, while features are largely comparable across apps, user experience is now the true differentiator.

BRAG conducted a deep heuristic and behavioral usability study of India's three biggest trading platforms:

  • Zerodha (India's largest discount broker)
  • Dhan (UX-first newer entrant)
  • Upstox (VC-backed with mass reach)

Methodology

Sample Size

150 participants (Tier 1 + Tier 2 cities)

Age Group

20–38 years

Tools Used

Maze, Useberry, Attention Insight, Lookback

Test Tasks

  • Buy a stock
  • Check P&L
  • Add funds

Heuristic Comparison

UX Takeaways & Patterns

Zerodha
Strengths:

Fastest task completion, cleanest visual hierarchy, few distractions

Weaknesses:
  • Poor for new users — Lack of visual onboarding
  • Hidden features need 3+ taps
"I know what I'm doing here — but I wouldn't give this to my dad to figure out."
Dhan
Strengths:

Clear discoverability, persistent help cues

Weaknesses:
  • Too many action buttons
  • Alert center UX secondary
"Nice design but feels a bit cramped. I like the help bubbles though."
Upstox
Strengths:

Friendly branding, great personalization potential

Weaknesses:
  • Highest error rate
  • Banner-heavy UI
"Why are there banners inside my trade screen? It's distracting."
