YouTube Channel UX Showdown

Comparative Research of India's Leading Finance Education Channels

Introduction

In the attention economy, where every second counts, YouTube channels have evolved beyond just content — they're now brands, interfaces, and businesses. In this report, BRAG analyzes and compares the UX, content structure, and viewer journey across India's three leading finance/education YouTube channels:

  • CA Rachana Phadke Ranade (Finance education for beginners)
  • Asset Yogi (Real estate and investing deep-dives)
  • Pranjal Kamra (Finology) (Behavioral finance and strategy)

The goal: Decode which channel maximizes clarity, trust, and retention — and how their UX design choices impact learning.

Methodology

Sample Size

250 viewers across India

Tools Used

TubeBuddy, Vidooly, YouTube Studio Analysis, Lookback, Attention Insight

Test Tasks

  • Navigate playlists
  • Find latest IPO video
  • Subscribe and enable alerts

UX Takeaways & Patterns

CA Rachana Phadke Ranade
Strengths:

Clean branding, excellent structure for beginners, high trust tone

Weaknesses:
  • Repetitive visuals in thumbnails
  • No deep-level segmentation by user type
"Feels like a course, not a channel — and that's great if I'm just starting."
Asset Yogi
Strengths:

Long-form evergreen content, excellent detail

Weaknesses:
  • Playlist UX is hard to follow
  • Content lacks beginner pathways
"I found great info… but had to search a lot before finding the right video."
Pranjal Kamra
Strengths:

Clear storytelling, strong call-to-action, personality-led trust

Weaknesses:
  • Inconsistent visuals
  • Comment moderation not always effective
"I don't just watch him — I trust his opinion."
